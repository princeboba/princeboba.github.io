Europe in Sight

Jessica Lo • October 10, 2019• Blogs

Europe is an absolutely beautiful place to visit! During 2019, I had the pleasure of visiting Europe for the first time, touring London, Edinburgh, and Paris from September 18th, 2019 to September 28th (10 days). Here are some details to get you started on your own trip!

Before you go:

  • Cell Service: I am nothing without my phone. Make sure your phone is unlocked so that you may purchase a European SIM card for data service while abroad. There are two options:
      (1) Purchased online with Amazon - 12GB for $21
      (2) Using an existing provider extension (call your own cell phone provider for rates)
  • Weather: Check the weather before you go! When we went, the weather ranged between 45 - 80 degrees F during our stay. It might get a bit rainy during various times of the year.
  • Theft: A lot of people get pickpocketed in Europe (especially Paris), so it’s recommended to get a money belt on you for valuables. Bring zippered bags and locks: People often get robbed (pickpocketed/mugged) multiple times within only a few days.
  • Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP): The Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP) is a free service to allow U.S. citizens and nationals travelling and living abroad to enroll their trip with the nearest U.S. Embassy. Enroll here (Passport info needed)
  • Notify your company: Some companies require notification before leaving the country, even on personal travel. Check your company policies to ensure compliance.
  • U.S. Travel Warnings: Unfortunately, destinations sometimes have travel warnings issued by the State Department. If it’s a popular Western European destination like the ones we visited, you should be fine, but these are good resources.
  • Print Budget Airline Tickets: If you are flying a budget airline, many of them require physical, printed tickets. For example, Ryanair requires tickets to be purchased and printed ahead of time for international travelers.
  • Add Tickets to Apple/Android Wallet: Add as many digital tickets as you can on your phone to avoid rummaging through your email, relying on spotty WiFi, or messing with physical copies.
  • Order your Oyster Card: London’s Visitor Oyster Card is a convenient way for tourists to travel London via “The Tube” for special discounted rates. See more information here .
      You must buy a Visitor Oyster card before you leave home and have it delivered to you. One benefit is that the pay-as-you-go fares are cheaper than buying a paper single ticket or Day Travelcard.
From Washington D.C. to London

We flew from Washington Dulles Airport to London Heathrow Airport via British Airways round trip in standard economy class. We enjoyed the seats, which were roomier than domestic flights with better recline for the long haul. Our plane wasn’t too crowded and we bought our tickets at a great price, so we were satisfied with the flights overall. If you also fly British Airways, make sure to add your Advanced Passenger Information (Global Entry ID, Passport Information, Residency) in “Manage my Booking” on the British Airways website before your trip. When booking, check out this helpful London Airport Guide by CNN

From London Heathrow Airport to Downtown London

We took the Heathrow Express train from LHW into Downtown London since it seemed to be the most economical, “British”, and seamless way to get into the city. From Paddington Station in London, we were able to quickly call a “cabbie” (British cab driver) to get to our hotel easily. Ubers also work, but we opted for cab for the British experience.

Details about the Heathrow Express to/from London Paddington Station: Train runs every 15 minutes and takes 15 minutes to reach London. We paid $88.20 for 3 tickets round-trip, or $29.40/person, which is a discounted rate for purchasing ahead of time.

  • Tickets are available via email (PDF) or can be placed on your wallet using Apple Pay
  • Trains run every 15 minutes from Terminals 2 and 3 (a few minutes more for Terminals 4 and 5)
  • Tickets are valid all-day, so there is no need to book ahead of time
Review: Ibis Blackfriars Hotel in London: 4/5 Stars

September 19th (Thursday) to September 21st (Saturday)

Price: We stayed at the ibis budget hotel in the Blackfriars neighborhood of London. In total, we paid $598.444 for two rooms, or $149.61 each between four people.

Blackfriars is a lovely neighborhood and extremely convenient to see many of the traditional London sights such as the London Eye and the Tower Bridge. Here’s a blurb from the hotel website: “Right on the doorstep of some of the world's most famous sights and attractions, ibis London Blackfriars promises comfort and convenience in abundance. Whether you're in the capital for some culture on the Southbank and a ride on the Eye, or for a business meeting over in the City, we're perfectly located. Nearby you'll enjoy some of London's best markets, independent stores, and live music venues, while crowd-pleasers like St Paul's Cathedral and Tate Modern are within easy reach.”

It was also very close to the District Line on the London Tube, which made it very convenient to get from place to place within the city.

We found ibis to be a decent hotel at an affordable rate. The rooms were small, but clean. The bathrooms were also small, which reminded me of airport bathrooms (except with a shower). There wasn’t any toiletries except for a 3-in-1 body wash/shampoo/conditioner solution and some lotion, but I’m glad I packed a small amount of each in my bag. If I didn’t the neighborhood was convenient, so I would have been able to purchase it as well. The mattress was on the harder side and felt a bit cheap, but I did still sleep soundly. The hotel was very affordable, so I have no major complaints overall and any small discomforts were warranted with price.

Review: Destiny Apartments in Edinburgh: 5/5 Stars

September 21st (Saturday) to September 23rd (Monday)

Edinburgh isn’t a huge city, so it’s not split into as many neighborhoods. For us, we stayed right in the middle of Downtown Edinburgh the old medieval Edinburgh Old Town and Georgian Edinburgh New Town. This allowed us to be in a central location to explore all that the city had to offer, from the sweeping hills, to the history, to the universities, to the new establishments in town.

We chose to stay at the Destiny Apartments in St. Andrew’s Square, booking two rooms for $573.20 total, $143.30 each for four people. The rooms were amazing for the price point, featuring large, lush beds and lovely furniture. The bathrooms were well equipped and newly remodeled - it definitely helped make our stay fabulous. Since this is a small hotel with only about 20 rooms, we felt like we were staying in a lovely apartment building while enjoying our time exploring. The entrance was right off the main road next to the McDonald’s, and it added some novelty to our trip to slip in through a staircase to live above the shops. There were plenty of stairs, but nothing that four, fit adults could not handle.

The small downside is that the check-in process required a keypad, and there is no reception in this building. All you need to check-in is your flat number and your 4 digit entry code, but when issues arose, you would have to call the main office, which closed after 5 pm.

Here’s an excerpt from the hotel website: “St Andrew Square Apartments provide a great central base from which to explore the city. In the bustling East End, the property is just a 2-minute walk from Waverley Railway Station and places you on the doorstep for everything that central Edinburgh has to offer. An ideal base for a bit of retail therapy at Harvey Nichols and Multrees Walk. The old town and Royal Mile are a 10-minutes by foot, and a wide range of restaurants, bars, cafes, and shops are located in the immediate vicinity of the apartments. Great transport links to the airport with a tram stop 100m away on St Andrew Square and an Airlink bus stop on George Street just a few minutes walk away.”

Review: Scottish Whiskey Experience: 5/5 Stars

The Scottish Whisky Experience is a must-go for most tourists to Edinburgh. The Gold Tour that we purchased includes both the Scottish Whisky Experience tour and whisky tastings. I never knew about all the various types of Scotch and which regions that they are from, so it was really fun to learn about that on a tour that was modern with many technology elements. The tour guides are educated and enthusiastic, and the end of the tour allows you to venture inside the room with the largest unopened Scotch collection in the world! Even as someone who doesn’t enjoy Scotch on the regular, I was mesmerized by all the history behind the bottles in the collection. I learned on the tour that whisky is spelled without an “e” in Scotland, but with an “e” when referring to Irish and American “whiskies”.

My friends who love drinking whisky would absolutely enjoyed the tour. You can even bring home a few bottles to share with family and friends.

Review: Paris Airbnb in Marais: 5/5 Stars

September 23rd (Monday) to September 25th (Wednesday)

We wanted to spend half of our trip in hotels, while the other half in quaint Airbnbs. This way, we would be able to experience our trip in two different ways - one in the comfort of a hotel, but another with the culture and experience behind a unique Airbnb. For Paris, we chose Superbe Appartement tres bien place in the Marais neighborhood within the 3rd Arrondissement of Paris, known for shopping, eclectic artists, dancers, and young folk.

About the Marais neighborhood: “The Marais could be compared to Manhattan’s SoHo neighborhood, with something for everyone. Luxury independent designer boutiques contrast the plethora of vintage shopping that sells second-hand goods by the kilo, as well as haute cuisine at Zagat celebrated restaurants versus local corner brasseries-turned hipster hangouts. Unfortunately, this neighborhood is notoriously pricey.”

There were two bedrooms and one bathroom, along with washer/dryer and a sofa bed. For our stay, we spent $528 total, or $132 each, which wasn’t bad for Paris. The gorgeous apartment was tucked behind a pair of beautiful blue doors next to shops in Paris. When you opened those beautiful blue doors, there was a peaceful courtyard, with beautiful French garden apartments covered in vines from a storybook. Every time I opened the doors to come home from a day of exploring Paris, I would be amazed by the beauty of this residence.

The owner met us at the residence to provide us with the keys and show us around the space. We were very pleased to find a large, remodeled kitchen, beautiful furniture, Parisian architecture, and some treats waiting for us inside. The host surprised us with a bottle of French red wine, biscuits, and a bowl of fruit which we were happy to enjoy during our stay. When we opened the windows, we could welcome the air into the apartment, and people watch outside the window at night. We also definitely took advantage of the washer/dryer. Throughout our stay, he was very quick to respond to our messages and eager to provide suggestions in the neighborhood. Each of the rooms were even smaller than our previous stays, but they were still lovely and to be expected from such a small Parisian apartment.

Plus, the location of this apartment was perfect. It was right next to the Republique Metro Station in the north of the neighborhood. There was a skate park right next to the metro entrance, full of teenagers enjoying themselves, and lively restaurants and music as you walk down the street. We loved this location so much, that I would not hesitate to stay here when I return to Paris in the future.

Review: London Airbnb in Seven Dials/Covent Garden: 4.5/5 Stars

September 25th (Wednesday) to September 28th (Saturday)

For the last leg of the trip, we wanted to pick a new neighborhood in London where we would be able to explore something different than we had the first time. As a result, we chose to stay in Covent Garden, specially at an area called the Seven Dials. We booked Seven Dials, elegant Covent Garden - sleeps 6 on Airbnb at our priciest rate yet - $1117.26 total, $279.32 per person.

Here’s a quick description of Covent Garden from another website: “Covent Garden in London's West End is a popular destination for visitors and Londoners who enjoy shopping, theatre, restaurants, bars, history and culture. Geographically, Covent Garden is located in Central London, bordered by the City of London and Leicester Square. It is within walking distance of Soho, the river Thames and Trafalgar Square.”

We were so happy to have booked such an amazing location for our last few days in Europe - this Airbnb really blew us away. It’s located right in the heart of the Seven Dials, literally 3 steps from the actual Seven Dials circle in Covent Garden. For reference, Seven Dials is a unique section of the Covent Garden neighborhood in London where 7 streets intersect at a beautiful statue in the middle. Of course this creates a traffic nightmare, but it creates the most wonderful area to shop, dine, and explore in the city. During the first part of our trip, we ventured to see all of the big London monuments, but staying in Covent Garden allowed us to see the modern trendy neighborhoods that London is so well-known for.

Our Airbnb was beautiful. It was just up the stairs next to a main restaurant in Seven Dials. After walking up the stairs, we would unlock the doors into the entrance and walk up a few more stairs into the main living area. Just like in Paris, we were amazed to live in a lovely garden apartment directly above all the popular coffee shops, pubs, restaurants, and stores below. The place itself was lovely, with two decently sized rooms with comfortable beds. The bathroom was new and remodeled, although the shower took a very long time to figure out how to operate. The living room was decently decorated, with plenty of familiar Ikea furniture. We did invite two friends who lived in London to our Airbnb to chat and hang out, and the living room provided a nice place to congregate. The place was quiet, clean, and in the best location possible. What really put the cherry on top was how we were able to explore, exercise, dine, and shop just minutes walking from the residence.
