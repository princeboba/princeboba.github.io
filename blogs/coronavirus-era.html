A New Era of Travel

George He • May 3, 2020• Blogs

Folks, we’re entering a new era of transportation. While the U.S. government has yet to issue guidance or mandates for personal protective equipment in travels, many U.S. airlines have taken the initiative to develop their own policies to help keep their employees and passengers safe, by limiting the potential for exposure.

Earlier this week, JetBlue, Alaska, American, Delta, Frontier, Hawaiian, Southwest and United announced a new requirement for passengers to wear face masks beginning in the first weeks of May.

Now, even Uber will be requiring face masks for both drivers and riders. Uber drivers and delivery workers will be required to wear some form of face covering, such as a mask or bandana. Uber said it is trying to get masks to drivers who need them as soon as possible, taking steps in April to begin shipping masks to drivers and delivery workers, as well as disinfectant spray.

Over the past months, airlines have already taken much-needed changes in how they operate by:

  • blocking seats
  • offering disinfectant wipes onboard
  • limiting sale of food and beverages onboard
  • enhancing cleaning measures between flights
  • offering free cancellations/changes to booked flights (with restrictions)
Mandatory face masks are one of the newest (and much needed) layers of protection for travelers and airline staff. With stay-at-home orders in place in many U.S. states, individuals should only travel if necessary.

The changes drew the support of Sara Nelson, president of the Association of Flight Attendants, who has been asking the federal government to intervene and make masks mandatory. There are still calls on the federal government - whether it be DOT, FAA, DHS, CDC - to issue uniform guidelines for all airlines and staff.
